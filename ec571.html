Key Summary

Simulated a 6T SRAM using a Hierarchical Bit-Line and Local Sense Amplifier (HBLSA) architecture. The HBLSA structure isolates high-capacitance bit-lines using local sense amplifiers to lower dynamic power and was directly compared against a conventional 6T SRAM design under identical test conditions.

HBLSA Architecture

- Hierarchical layout minimizes bit-line capacitance.
- Local Word Line (LWL) selects specific memory cells within a small group.
- Global Word Line (GWL) decouples unused groups, conserving dynamic power.
- Local Sense Amplifiers (LSAs) amplify sub-bit-line signals during read and write operations.

Simulation Results

Write Operation
- HBLSA SRAM: 60 µA vs Conventional: 90 µA
- Power savings: 33.33%

Read Operation
- HBLSA SRAM: 16 µA vs Conventional: 35 µA
- Power savings: 54.29%

Conclusion

The HBLSA-SRAM demonstrated significant dynamic power reduction while maintaining full-swing operation and acceptable noise margins. The hierarchical bit-line design proved effective for scaling SRAM arrays in low-power systems where energy efficiency is critical.
