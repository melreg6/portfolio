Project Overview

Developed a Pratt-style truss bridge using joint method analysis and matrix-based force computation. Designed two truss variations and evaluated load distribution, buckling risk, and load-to-cost efficiency using both code and manual calculations.

Truss Calculation Method

Used the joint method with RREF matrix computations to solve for internal member forces. Negative force values indicated compression, while positive values indicated tension. Manual and code-based approaches were cross-validated for accuracy.

Results & Comparison

Design 1: Max load = 36.87 ± 1.356 oz  |  Load-to-cost ratio = 0.19 oz/$
Design 2: Max load = 31.26 ± 1.849 oz  |  Load-to-cost ratio = 0.165 oz/$

Both designs used 8 joints and approximately 110 inches of member length. Design 1 was selected due to its higher load capacity and better overall efficiency.

Conclusion

The Pratt truss design proved most effective for balancing strength and material cost. To further optimize, we recommend reducing member lengths through slight node position adjustments. Computer analysis improved accuracy and made it feasible to rapidly test multiple design iterations.
